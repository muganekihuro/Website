welcome to Vancouver island

Explore lush wilderness parks, where hiking trails lead to breathtaking vistas and encounters with native wildlife, including orcas and black bears. The island's coastline is adorned with softly curving beaches, providing a serene backdrop against the backdrop of distant, snow-capped mountains. Beyond its natural wonders, Vancouver Island boasts cosmopolitan centers, charming small towns, and welcoming harbors that invite visitors to unwind and immerse themselves in the island's unique charm. Whether seeking outdoor thrills or a tranquil escape, Vancouver Island promises a memorable journey filled with beauty, exploration, and the warmth of island hospitality.

PLACES TO VISIT

North island

Beginning just an hour north of Campbell River and stretching towards Sayward, the North Island region unfolds to the windswept tip at Cape Scott, encompassing the rugged Pacific coast and extending east to the whale-rich waters, including sections of the Great Bear Rainforest and BC mainland. Top of the wishlist for visitors are whale and bear sightings, and the region offers group outings on comfortable cabin cruisers or thrilling open-air Zodiacs in pursuit of orcas, humpback whales, porpoises, Steller sea lions, Minke whales, and harbour seals. The prime season from May to October is ideal for nautical excursions from Northern Vancouver Island to the mainland's grizzly bear habitats.

ABOUT VANCOUVER ISLAND

Nestled on Canada's West Coast, Vancouver Island spans 460 kilometers from Victoria to Cape Scott Provincial Park. This idyllic island is a tapestry of ancient rainforests, rugged coastlines, and the Pacific's cool embrace. A haven for outdoor enthusiasts, it offers kayaking, lush trails, and serene beaches. Beyond nature's allure, Vancouver Island's vibrant communities contribute to its cultural richness and laid-back lifestyle, creating a harmonious blend of coastal living and natural splendor.
